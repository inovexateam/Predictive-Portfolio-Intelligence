What each strategy tests

EMA Pullback

Stock in uptrend (above SMA50 + SMA200). Price pulls back to SMA21. RSI cools to 40–60. First close back above SMA21 with volume = entry. Classic trend-continuation play.

ORB — Opening Range Breakout

Marks the high and low of the first 30 min after open. Waits for a candle to close above the high with 1.5× average volume. Most powerful on gap-up days with Nifty also trending up.

VWAP Bounce

Price touches VWAP from above (a healthy pullback in an uptrend) then closes back above it with expanding volume. Best used between 10 AM and 1:30 PM.

Bollinger Squeeze

Bollinger Band width reaches its 6-month narrowest point (energy stored). 3+ inside bars visible. A breakout above the range with 2× average volume triggers the entry.

Order Block Entry

Finds the last red daily candle before a strong 3%+ up-move. When price returns to that candle's body zone, it dips briefly below (liquidity grab) then recovers. That recovery is the entry.

Bear Trap (Failed Breakdown)

Price breaks below a major support level that held 3+ times. Retail panic-sells. But within 1–2 candles price closes back above the level. All sellers are now trapped. Entry on recovery.

Post-Earnings Drift

After a strong earnings beat (EPS +10%, revenue beat, guidance raised), waits 2–3 days for initial sellers to exit. Buys the first close above 9-EMA with rising volume. Rides institutional upgrades.

Sector Catch-Up

Sector has moved 5%+ driven by FII buying. One quality stock in the sector has barely moved (1–2%). When that stock closes above SMA21 with volume, it should catch up to its peers.

Position sizing — the exact formula

Every trade uses risk-based sizing. The same risk % is applied regardless of the stock's price level or volatility. ATR adjusts the stop automatically.

Risk amount

Capital × Risk% = maximum loss on this trade. E.g. ₹5,00,000 × 1% = ₹5,000 max risk.

Stop distance

ATR(14) × ATR multiplier = distance from entry to stop in rupees. E.g. ₹18 × 1.5 = ₹27 stop.

Quantity

Risk Amount ÷ Stop Distance = shares to buy. E.g. ₹5,000 ÷ ₹27 = 185 shares. Never guessed.

Three targets

T1 = Entry + (Stop × R:R × 0.6). T2 = Entry + (Stop × R:R). T3 = trailed until stopped.

What the metrics mean

Profit Factor

Total gross profit ÷ Total gross loss. Above 1.5 = good system. Above 2.0 = excellent. Below 1.0 = losing money overall.

Expectancy

(Win rate × Avg win%) − (Loss rate × Avg loss%). If positive, the system makes money over many trades regardless of any single outcome.

Sharpe ratio

Return relative to risk taken. Above 1.0 = good. Below 0.5 = too much risk for the return. Estimated here as return / (max drawdown × √trades).

Max drawdown

The worst peak-to-trough equity drop during the test period. Keep this below 15%. If it breaches the monthly limit you set, the engine stops trading for that month.

Important — what this does NOT test

Not a specific stock

Data is synthetic. It has realistic volatility and trend but is not RELIANCE, INFY, or any NSE stock. Use this to validate strategy logic, not to predict a specific stock.

Not real market microstructure

Real NSE has circuit breakers, gaps on news, illiquid bid-ask spreads, and block deal impacts that this simulation cannot replicate.

Parameter Optimisation

Sweep one parameter at a time across a range. Each button runs multiple backtests and plots the result so you can find the optimal value for your strategy and market condition.

Risk % vs Total Return + Drawdown

Sweeps risk from 0.25% to 3%. Shows where return peaks before drawdown becomes dangerous. Your ideal risk sits just before the drawdown accelerates.

R:R Target vs Win Rate + Return

Sweeps R:R from 1:1 to 1:5. Higher targets mean fewer wins but bigger ones. Find the cross-over where expectancy is highest.

Min Confirmations vs Trade Count + Return

Sweeps confirmations from 1 to 5. More confirmations = fewer but higher-quality trades. Shows the quality-vs-quantity trade-off.

ATR Multiplier vs Return + Drawdown

Sweeps ATR stop multiplier from 0.5× to 3×. Tighter stops = more losers but smaller losses. Find where return and drawdown balance.
