Complete trader's guide — all indicators, rules, and mindset principles

Trend indicators
SMA 50 / 200Trend
The most-watched moving averages. Price above both = uptrend. Golden Cross (50 crosses above 200) is a strong buy signal.
📈 Buy zone: price > SMA50 > SMA200
📉 Danger: price < SMA50 < SMA200
EMA 9 / 21Trend
Faster moving averages — react quicker to price changes. Used for short-term and intraday trades.
⚡ EMA9 crossing above EMA21 = momentum building
MACDTrend
Shows momentum + direction. MACD line crossing above signal = buy. Histogram shrinking = momentum fading.
✅ Buy: MACD line > signal line
❌ Sell: MACD line < signal line
ADXTrend
Measures trend strength, not direction. Combine with +DI/-DI lines to confirm direction.
Strong trend: ADX > 25 | Weak/sideways: < 20
Momentum indicators
RSIMomentum
Most popular oscillator. Scale 0–100. Watch for divergence — price new high, RSI doesn't = hidden weakness.
Overbought: >70 | Oversold: <30
Sweet spot to buy: 40–60
StochasticMomentum
Compares close to recent range. Best in sideways markets. %K and %D crossovers signal turns.
Overbought: >80 | Oversold: <20
ROCMomentum
Measures how fast price is moving. Good for spotting acceleration before a breakout.
Rising ROC = increasing momentum
Williams %RMomentum
Measures overbought/oversold relative to recent high. Useful for reversal timing.
Overbought: -20 to 0 | Oversold: -80 to -100
Volatility indicators
Bollinger BandsVolatility
Three bands: middle SMA + upper/lower at 2 std deviations. Band squeeze = big move coming. Price touching outer band = extreme zone.
Band squeeze → watch for breakout direction
ATRVolatility
Average True Range — measures volatility, not direction. Essential for setting stop loss distance based on market conditions.
Stop loss = entry − (1.5 × ATR)
India VIXVolatility
Fear gauge of the market. Low VIX = calm. High VIX = panic — options are expensive, avoid fresh longs.
Safe: <15 | Caution: 20–25 | Panic: >25
Volume indicators
OBVVolume
Cumulative volume tells if smart money is flowing in or out. OBV rising with price = healthy. OBV falling while price rises = distribution — danger.
OBV divergence = early warning signal
Volume spikeVolume
2–3× average volume = institutional activity. On a breakout = confirmation. On a reversal day = trend change.
No volume on breakout = false breakout risk
VWAPVolume
Volume Weighted Average Price — intraday benchmark. Institutions buy near VWAP. Price above = bullish intraday tone.
Buy near VWAP support | Sell near VWAP resistance
Fundamental indicators
P/E RatioFundamental
Compare to sector average. Nifty 50 avg is ~20–22. Above 24 = market expensive. Low P/E could be value or a trap — check why.
Always compare to sector peers, not market P/E
ROEFundamental
Return on Equity. Above 15% consistently = well-managed. Buffett looks for ROE >20% for 10 consecutive years.
Good: >15% | Excellent: >20% sustained
EPS growthFundamental
Consistent 15–20%+ quarterly EPS growth = quality stock. Sudden EPS drop = red flag. Compare to analyst estimates.
Beat estimates = stock likely moves up next day
Debt / EquityFundamental
Below 1 is generally safe. High debt + rising interest rates = dangerous. Avoid highly leveraged companies in rate hike cycles.
Alert: D/E > 2 in rising rate environment
Promoter holdingFundamental
Above 50% = promoters are confident. Pledged promoter shares = major red flag. Watch quarterly promoter holding changes.
⚠️ Pledging increasing = exit signal
P/B RatioFundamental
Below 1 = trading below asset value. Best for banking stocks. High P/B only acceptable with high ROE.
Banking stocks: P/B < 1.5 = value zone
Market-wide signals (India)
FII / DII dataMacro
Foreign Institutional buying = strong bullish signal. FII selling heavily = caution. Check daily on NSE website. DII buying can cushion FII selling.
FII net buy > ₹2000Cr = strong market support
PCR (Put-Call)Macro
Contrarian indicator. Too many puts = market may bounce. Too many calls = market may fall. Best for Nifty/BankNifty.
PCR > 1.2 = oversold | PCR < 0.8 = overbought
52-week high / lowMacro
Near 52w high = strong resistance. Breakout above with volume = very bullish. Near 52w low = potential support or value trap.
Breakout above 52w high + volume = buy signal
Pre-trade entry checklist — need 4+ ticked
1Price is above SMA 50 and SMA 200 — confirms uptrend is intact
2RSI is between 40–65 — not overbought, momentum building but room to run
3MACD line is above signal line — bullish momentum confirmed
4Volume above 20-day average on the entry candle — institutional interest present
5EPS growing 15%+ for 2+ quarters — fundamental backing for the move
6Promoter pledge is low or decreasing — insiders are not under pressure
7VIX is below 18 — market is calm enough for positional trades
8FII is net buying that week — foreign money flowing into the market
9Stock near sector high-relative strength — outperforming its own sector is a strong signal
10Risk:Reward is at least 1:2 — potential gain is 2× the stop loss distance before entering
Risk management rules every trader must follow
1% capital risk per trade
Never risk more than 1% of total capital on a single trade. On a ₹10L portfolio, max loss per trade = ₹10,000. This keeps you alive through 10 consecutive losses.
Always set stop loss before entry
Decide stop loss BEFORE entering — not after. Use ATR-based stops (entry − 1.5×ATR) or below the nearest support. Never move stop loss down to avoid a loss.
Minimum 1:2 risk-to-reward ratio
If your stop is ₹20, your target must be at least ₹40. With a 50% win rate and 1:2 R:R you're still profitable. Many traders ignore this — most losing traders do.
Max 6% portfolio drawdown rule
If portfolio is down 6% in a month — stop trading for the rest of the month. Review all open trades. This protects from emotional revenge trading spirals.
Max 10–15% in one stock, 25% in one sector
Concentration risk kills portfolios silently. Even if you're very confident — cap single-stock exposure. One accounting scandal can wipe 50–80% overnight.
What else a trader must know
Market timings
Pre-open: 9:00–9:15 AM. Market: 9:15 AM–3:30 PM. First 30 min and last 30 min are most volatile — avoid inexperienced trades then. F&O expiry: last Thursday of the month.
Order types
Market order fills instantly at any price. Limit order fills only at your price. SL-M order = stop loss market. Always use limit orders for large quantities to avoid slippage eating your profit.
Taxes & costs
Short-term gains (held <1yr) taxed at 20% in India. Long-term gains >₹1.25L taxed at 12.5%. STT, brokerage, GST, stamp duty add up — calculate all costs before a trade is worth taking.
Events calendar
RBI policy dates, quarterly results, Union Budget, US Fed meetings — all cause major moves. Never hold large positions before these without a hedge. Check the economic calendar weekly.
Trading psychology
Fear and greed are the two enemies. Keep a trading journal — write reason for entry, exit, and emotion at the time. Most losses come from not following your own rules, not from wrong analysis.
Candlestick patterns
Doji = indecision. Hammer = reversal from bottom. Shooting star = reversal from top. Engulfing = strong reversal. Always confirm with volume. Patterns on weekly charts are more reliable than daily.
Support & resistance
S/R are price levels where buyers and sellers repeatedly clash. Round numbers (₹500, ₹1000, ₹2000) are strong psychological levels. Previous all-time highs become resistance. Previous highs become support after a breakout.
Macro awareness
Crude oil affects inflation and transport stocks. Dollar-rupee affects IT exporters and import-heavy sectors. Interest rate cuts = good for banks and real estate. Always know the macro backdrop of your trade.
